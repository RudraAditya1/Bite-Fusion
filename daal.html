Daal Kachori

Dal Kachori is a popular snack in Bihar, made by stuffing a crispy, deep-fried pastry with a spicy and flavorful lentil (dal) filling. The lentil filling is typically made with split yellow peas (chana dal) or pigeon peas (toor dal), which are cooked with spices like cumin, coriander, turmeric, and red chili powder. details

Dal Kachori is a popular snack in Bihar, made by stuffing a crispy, deep-fried pastry with a spicy and flavorful lentil (dal) filling. The lentil filling is typically made with split yellow peas (chana dal) or pigeon peas (toor dal), which are cooked with spices like cumin, coriander, turmeric, and red chili powder. The cooked lentils are then mashed and mixed with aromatic spices to create a thick, flavorful mixture. In Bihar, Dal Kachori is often enjoyed during festivals, special occasions, or as a tea-time snack. It is served hot with a variety of accompaniments such as tamarind chutney, mint chutney, or a spicy tomato-based curry. The dish is not only delicious but also nutritious, as lentils are a good source of protein, fiber, and essential nutrients.
